# Company Research — Ramp

## Basics

- **Stage:** Series D fintech unicorn.
- **Valuation (user-specified anchor):** **$5.8B+** at last confirmed round. (Note: public reporting has suggested higher marks at subsequent rounds; confirm current 409A / round data during recruiter screen before negotiating equity.)
- **Co-founder / CEO:** **Eric Glyman**. Co-founder / CTO: Karim Atiyeh.
- **Headquarters:** **New York City**. Secondary office: San Francisco. Culture is described as remote-friendly but with a strong in-office expectation for PMs (2+ days/week).
- **Scale:** $100B+ annualized spend processed across 50K+ customer companies (last public signal).

## Product

Integrated finance stack for SMB and mid-market:
- **Corporate cards** (core flagship product).
- **Spend management** — policy enforcement, receipt capture, categorization.
- **Bill pay** — AP automation, vendor management, approval workflows.
- **Accounting automation** — GL sync (QuickBooks, NetSuite, Sage Intacct).
- Adjacent: treasury, procurement, travel, risk.

## Competitors

- **Brex** — most direct competitor on corporate cards + spend; historically more enterprise-skewed.
- **Mercury** — banking-adjacent, SMB overlap.
- **Airbase** — acquired by **Paylocity** (absorbed into a payroll / HR suite).
- **Divvy** — acquired by **Bill.com** (absorbed into Bill's AP stack).

The consolidation pattern leaves Ramp and Brex as the two scaled independents in the SMB-spend category, with traditional AP providers (Bill.com, Coupa) circling.

## Culture

- **Builder-first.** JD explicitly calls out "builders who own problems end to end" and high-velocity execution.
- **High bar for product thinking.** Interview loops test whether you can reason from user need to shipped surface without hand-holding.
- **Ownership-heavy.** PMs own a product area across the full stack (discovery → launch → measurement → iteration), not a narrow slice.
- Small pods: PM + engineers + designers. Not matrix orgs.
- Rapid prototyping normalized (Cursor, Bolt, Claude Code named in the JD).
